Missing Children Minnesota

Our Story

Missing Children Minnesota is a 501(c)(3) non-profit, one of the oldest child search agencies in the country, and the oldest in the Midwest. MCM began in 1983 as a support group for parents of missing children to offer moral support and comfort to each other during their ordeal. Out of that group came the idea there should be a Minnesota-based resource for parents and families dealing with the issue of missing children.

Since its inception, we have helped thousands of families with information, support and referral services in their search for their missing children. We have educated thousands of children, parents, educators, and other professionals on the subject of personal safety for children. We have functioned as a resource for information to the public on the issue of missing children.

Missing Children Minnesota is a Charter Member of the Association of Missing and Exploited Children’s Organizations (AMECO) and a Member Organization of Community Shares of Minnesota (CSMN).

Our Mission

The Mission of Missing Children Minnesota is to assist in locating missing children; provide advocacy and support services to families of missing children; and to provide information and education to the public about missing children, runaways, and abduction prevention.

Our History

In 1983, we participated in the re-writing of Minnesota’s parental kidnapping statute SS609.26.

In 1984, this group of parents incorporated as the Missing Kids Action Agency. The name was changed to Missing Children Minnesota in 1986.

In 1990, two delegates from MCM were invited to serve on Governor Rudy Perpich’s Governor’s Task Force on Missing Children.

In 1991, the legislature passed the Missing Children’s Act, which included registration of sex offenders, flagging of school records, and additional rewriting of the parental kidnapping statute.

Missing Children Minnesota continues to provide thousands of families with information, support and referral services in their search for their missing children.

Our Affiliations

AMECO

Missing Children Minnesota is a charter member of the Association of Missing and Exploited Children’s Organizations (AMECO).

As a Member of AMECO, we pledge to abide by the following:

  • Avoid even the appearance of any criminal offense or professional misconduct.
  • Accurately state our experience, qualifications and expertise.
  • Not disclose privileged information to unauthorized parties.
  • Take care that all solicitation materials are accurate and correctly reflect the organization’s mission and use of solicited funds.
  • Comply with all applicable local, state, provincial and federal civil and criminal law.
  • Treat the missing child and family members in a most humane, respectful, responsible, and non-degrading manner.
  • Verify all paperwork, warrants, and documents without which might lead to unnecessary harm to innocent persons.
  • Act in a manner of conduct that will bring credit to the cause of missing and exploited children, ourselves, the Not for Profit Organization (NPO) we represent, and our Association.
  • Report all facts developed in a case in a prompt and timely manner in order to assist law enforcement in the recovery of the child and the apprehension of any known suspects.

We must renew membership annually with an Annual Membership Report detailing our work in the communities of Minnesota.
